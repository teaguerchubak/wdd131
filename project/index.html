Who is Teaguer Chubak?

I was introduced to the SLC punk community around the year 2007, when my brother took me to a crowded house show. It was indescribable and I fell in love with the experience. Ever since then, I have been heavily involved in the local music scene. For the first couple years I simply attended shows, but starting in 2009 I began writing my own music and performing at them. Also in 2009 I began an internship at Spyhop where I worked in a recording studio learning the production side of music. Around that same time, one of my best friends opened his own venue, and I began volunteering time helping at his venue. Over the years, I have worked or performed at countless venues and studios, from small punk houses to large concert halls, but my favorite shows are always the intimate house shows with diy bands and close friends.

Listen to the Musn'ts

In December 2008, my friend Harper suggested starting a band with me. I said that'd be cool, but all I've got were a bunch of percussion instruments. They said that they also had a bunch of percussion instruments. We joked about how cool it would be to start a one-man-band style project, where we each played a bunch of different percussion instruments. At least, I thought we were joking, until New Year's Day when we were walking to a show and they told me they booked a show for February 6th and we were on the lineup. That was the moment I realized we were actually starting a band, and that we only had two months to write an entire set of songs. Aside from a couple songs that we only played at our first show, we were an entirely instrumental band. We played a few shows together over the span of a couple years, our last show being a big New Year's event at The Underground. We officially broke up when Harper moved away for school.

Spobo and the Sing Alongs

Spobo and the Sing Alongs started in the summer of 2009. This was my (mostly) solo project, where I played and sang guitar. I'm autistic, and have always struggled with expressing my thoughts. Initially I used this project as a way of venting. I didnt expect anyone to want to listen to my angry ramblings, but many friends did. And over time I learned how to write on more positive and fun themes, my favorite being traveling. This project continued on until 2019, when I decided to start over and rebrand as Petr Chubak.

Idyll Rigamarole

Idyll Rigamarole was a 7-person band that started as an internship at Spyhop. We all tried out for a music internship and were selected to be in a band together, where we learned about writing, recording and performing music from our mentor, Jeremy Chatelain. We were fortunate enough to perform on KRCL, last.FM, and Park City TV, as well as at Rose Wagner Theater. Our internship was for the school year 2009-10, but we continued playing shows together for a few months afterward, our final show being at a house called The Brigantine.

The Uniswarm Collective

The Uniswarm Collective started in 2011, and was originally myself and Tyler Espinoza. We both played ukulele and guitar and sang. After our first show, our friend Julie joined us on bass and guitar. We played numerous times throughout 2011 and 2012, the last time we played together being our album release show at the Bike House. When Tyler moved to Arizona for school, Julie and I decided to end the project.

Petr Chubak

Petr Chubak started in 2019 when I decided to start over with my solo music. Spobo and the Sing Alongs was largely songs I wrote as an angry and depressed teenager, and I no longer felt comfortable performing those songs at nearly 30 years old. My solo music now takes a more mellow sound, often addressing the theme of mental health with a more positive outlook. At least that's the idea, but we'll see where things go from here.
